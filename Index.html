About This Mode
CAD — Computer-Aided Design

Solid 3D model generated in CATIA V5. Every surface, dimension, and tolerance is digitally defined. This model feeds directly into CAM for manufacturing and CAE for simulation.

Engineering Wireframe

The underlying mesh structure — the skeleton of the 3D model. CAE solvers use this mesh to divide the structure into finite elements for mathematical analysis.

Wiring Harness Design

Electrical circuit routing through the vehicle structure. Each colour represents a different circuit: power, chassis ground, CAN bus, sensor signals, and lighting.

FEA — Crash Simulation

Von Mises stress distribution during a 64km/h frontal impact. Red zones exceed yield strength — intentional crumple zones absorb kinetic energy before it reaches the cabin.

CFD — Aerodynamic Flow

Computational Fluid Dynamics: streamlines coloured by velocity. Blue = slow (stagnation). Red = fast (low pressure). Used to minimise drag, lift, and improve fuel efficiency.
